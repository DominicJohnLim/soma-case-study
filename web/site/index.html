02

The audit question, answered with proof

"Show me every action and data source that contributed to the decision to invest in Company X."
The system answers with a proof bundle - records, log proofs, identity certificates, an external timestamp - and a standalone verifier checks all of it while trusting only the organization's CA key and the external notary's key. Not the log. Not the database. Not the operator.

04

What this proves - and what it honestly doesn't

PROVES
  • Who produced every artifact, under which certificate, on whose delegated authority
  • Exactly which inputs each step consumed, by content hash
  • That nothing was modified, deleted, or reordered after the fact - including by administrators, once anchored
  • That the full lineage is present: hiding even a failed attempt breaks verification
DOES NOT PROVE
  • That the AI was right - a hallucination gets recorded with perfect integrity
  • That external data sources told the truth - only what they served us, when
  • When a record was created beyond the anchor's bound - record times are self-claimed

These boundaries are structural, stated up front, and mitigated by requiring human approval records on every decision that matters.
